Mobelish Diphthongs

What is a diphthong? A diphthong is a sound formed by a combination of two vowels. An English diphthong example would be the word "lie." The combination of the vowels "i" and "e" create a different sound than just pronouncing them next to each other. There are not many Mobelish diphthongs, but let's take a look at them.

Genders

The language of Mobelish has three genders. The masculine gender, the feminine gender, and the neutral gender. In their normal states, masculine nouns end in -ak, feminine nouns end in -om, and neuter nouns end in -el. All three genders decline differently.

Noun Declension

What is declension? Declension is the state of changing a word based on how it's used. The declension of verbs is called conjugation. A good example of declension in English are the words "he", "him", and "his".

In Mobelish, nouns decline based on plurality and for the inclusion of the articles: "a", "an", "some", and "the".

For example, the word man - qresak - if you want to change it to the man, you have to add "ht" to make it definate (the man). It really is that simple!

Verb Conjugation

Full Mobelish Conjugation Chart

Noun declension was very simple, however, verb conjugation is not as simple. There are many conjugations, some including conjugated forms of other verbs. Mobelish has ten pronouns:

  • I - Omi
  • You - Ech
  • He - Tupa
  • She - Tupo
  • It - Tupe
  • We - Omhki
  • You/Y'all - Ecalh
  • They (m) - Tupoko
  • They (f) - Tuphmi
  • They (n) - Tupalo

    For explanations, we are going to use the verb "fenzap," meaning to eat. The root of the verb is "fenz", and we are going to replace the -ap with other letters to conjugate. Let's learn to say "I eat". To conjugate the verb, you add -i to the root, creating the word "fenzi." Now, you say the pronoun and then the verb, or you can just say the verb. Since the conjugation is different for each pronoun, you don't need to include the pronoun. However, if the subject is not a pronoun, but rather a noun instead, then you CANNOT take it out, like "the cat" or the combination of a noun and pronoun like "Mark and I."
